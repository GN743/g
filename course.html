Our Courses

Courses We Offer

Currently in Aiyura National School of Excellence there are three courses we offer. STEM, Block Science, and Social Science.

We offer Social Science as one of our major academic pathways, providing students with opportunities to explore a wide range of subjects and future careers.
We offer Block Science for students who are interested in scientific learning and wish to pursue science-related fields after school. We offer STEM to equip
students with the knowledge and skills needed foe higher education and careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

STEM

These mainly include the STEM courses like STEM Physics, STEM Biology, STEM Chemistry, Engineering, Technology, STEM Mathematics, and English. STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It is a broad and highly important field that prepares students for careers involving scientific discovery, technological innovation, engineering, mathematics, and problem-solving.

STEM provide students with a strong foundation for studying many different university degrees, just like Block Science would. But the difference with STEM is that students may continue into fields such as Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Computer Science, Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Information Technology, Data Science, Robotics, Physics, Mathematics, Environmental Science, and other scientific and technological fields, and the opportunities for scholarships is very broad.

Students who successfully complete STEM may have opportunities to study at universities in Papua New Guinea and overseas. In PNG, possible pathways include institutions such as the University of Papue New Guinea, Papua New Guinea University of Technology, and other universities and cplleges offering science, engineering, technology, and mathematics programmes.

Internationally, PNG students may also apply to universities in countries such as Australia, New Zealand, the United States, India, China, Japan, Germany, France, the United Kingdom, and many other countries. Examples of possible institutions include the University of North Dakota and the University of South Dakota in the United States, leading universities in India, and universities in Auatralia.

Block Science

These mainly include the science USSCE subjects like biology, chemistry, physics, advanced mathametics, and english. These subjects help students develop scientific kknowledge, mathematical reasoning, problem-solving skills, and an understanding of the natural world.

Unlike Social Sciencce, Block Science doe not have separate substreams and students who stream into Block Science get to take up all of the science subjects with Mathematics and English. This combination is useful for students who are interested in careers that require strong scientific and mathametical knowledge.

Students who study Block Science can pursue manydifferent opportunities after completing secondary school. In Papua New Guinea they may continue their education in fields such as medicine, nursing, pharmacy, environmental science, agriculture, laboratory science, engineering, information technology, and other science-related areas. The subjects studied in Block Science can also provide a strong foundation for students who wish to enter universities and colleges in PNG like Unitech, Polytech, Divine Word,etc.

Block Science is particularly valuable because Papue New Guinea has a growing need for professionals in areas such as healthcare, environmental protection, agriculture, mining, research, and science education.

Overall, Block Science provides students with a broad foundation in Science and Mathematics. It allows them to develop important skills such as critical thinking, logical reasoning, problem-solving, observation, and scientific analysis, which can be useful in many different careers and further areas of study.

Social Science

Currently, under Social Science there are three further sub-streams:

  1. General Social Science
  2. Block Social Science
  3. Business Studies

The first one is General Social Science , where students take Political Science, History, Econimics, General Mathametics, and English. This course focuses on governance, historical analysis, and socio-economic systems. People who take this course can become policy analysts, public administrators, political consultants, etc.

The second one is Block Social Science, where students take up Geography, History, Economics, General Maths, and English. This course focuses on spatial data, environmental systems, and human geography. People who take this course may become demographerss, urban planners, environmental consultants, etc.

The third and final one is Business. In this stream students study Business Studies, Economics, ICT, General Maths/Advanced Maths, and English. This course focuses on financial management, corporate strategy, and digital systems. People who take this course may become economists, fnancial analysts, data analysts, etc.

Generally, students going intothe Social Science stream maybe selected into universtities like UPNG, IBSU, Divine Word, UOG, and even unitech to further their studies. Social Science provides students with valuable opportunities to understand society, human behaviour, government, economics, and the world around them. These skills can lead to careers in law, business, politics, economics, public administration, education, and community development.

Our Facilities

Our school facilities include a school mess, te boys dorms, four girls dorms, classrooms, school library, a computer lab, three science labs, a STEM building, a multipurpose hall,
an Audio Visual Theatre, a Basketball court, a Rugby field, a Soccer field, and a small Aidpost to help students who are sick. These facilities provide students with a safe,
comfortable, and enjoyable learning environment. They support both academic studiesand extracurricular activities, allowing students to develop their knowledge, skills,
and talents.

School Library

This is the Aiyura NSOE School Library. It provides students with a quiet and comfortable place to read, study, and carry out research for their academic work.

School Mess

This is the Aiyura NSOE School Mess. It provides students with a comfortable space to enjoy their meals and build friendships with fellow students.

Boys Dorms

Further up are some of the boys' dormitories. They are an important part of campus life, giving students a place to live, connect with others, and develop independence and responsibility while studying at the school.

Multi-purpose Hall

This is the school's multipurpose hall. It is normally used for huge gatherings or programs hosted by the school like graduation, end of term crusades, assemblies, and more.

School Audio Visual Theatre, 'AVT'.

This is where students normally have assemblies on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays. Mondays and Fridays are combined assembly but on Wednesdays the boys use the AVT and the girls use the mess.

Aid Post

This is where students get medical assistance. If it is a serious case,or if the school is short of medical supplies then students are referred from here to Kainantu or to the nearest clinic at SIL, Ukarumpa.

School Administration Building

This is the Aiyura NSOE School Administration Building. If you want to enquire for something then you can go to the receptionist's section.

Basketball Court

This is the school's basketball court. Every afternoon students play basketball here at the court. Normally the game stops before or during mess time at 5pm.

Girls Dorms

This an Aiyura NSOE girls' dorms. The girls' dorms are highly secure because it has a fence around it. There is a gatekeeper for the girls who opens and locks the gate for the girls at a certain time in the morning and in the night.
